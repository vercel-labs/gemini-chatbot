# E.U.Z HSE Safety Procedures

## 1. Permit-to-Work (PTW) System

The E.U.Z Permit-to-Work system controls high-risk activities on all sites.

**Permit Types:**
- **Hot Work Permit** — Welding, cutting, grinding, or any work producing sparks near flammable materials.
- **Cold Work Permit** — General maintenance not involving hot work or electrical systems.
- **Electrical Isolation Permit** — Any work on live or de-energised electrical systems.
- **Confined Space Entry Permit** — Entry into tanks, vessels, or enclosed structures.
- **Excavation Permit** — Any ground-breaking or trenching activity.

**PTW Process:**
1. Requestor identifies the task and completes the permit application.
2. Area authority reviews hazards and approves or rejects the permit.
3. Performing authority acknowledges the permit and briefs the work party.
4. Work commences only after permit is signed by all parties.
5. On completion, permit is closed out and filed.

## 2. Lock-Out / Tag-Out (LOTO)

LOTO is mandatory before working on any energy-isolating equipment.

**Steps:**
1. Notify affected personnel.
2. Identify all energy sources (electrical, pneumatic, hydraulic, thermal).
3. Shut down the equipment using normal stopping procedure.
4. Isolate the energy source(s).
5. Apply personal lock(s) and tag(s) to each isolation point.
6. Release or restrain stored energy (bleed pressure, drain fluids, block gravity loads).
7. Verify the equipment is de-energised before starting work.
8. On completion: remove locks, re-energise, and notify all affected personnel.

## 3. Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)

**Mandatory site PPE (all personnel):**
- Hard hat (EN397 or equivalent)
- High-visibility vest (EN ISO 20471 Class 2+)
- Safety boots (EN ISO 20345 S3)
- Safety glasses (EN166)

**Task-specific PPE:**
- Face shield — grinding, chemical handling
- Hearing protection — noise zones (>85 dB)
- Chemical-resistant gloves — hazardous substance handling
- Full-body harness — work at height (>2 m)
- Respirator — confined spaces, spray painting, asbestos-adjacent work

## 4. Incident Reporting

All incidents, near-misses, and unsafe conditions must be reported within 24 hours.

**Categories:**
- **Near Miss** — Unsafe act or condition with no injury or damage.
- **First Aid** — Injury treated on-site with no lost time.
- **Medical Treatment** — Injury requiring treatment beyond first aid.
- **Lost Time Injury (LTI)** — Injury resulting in at least one day lost from work.
- **Dangerous Occurrence** — Structural collapse, explosion, or major release.

**Reporting Channel:** Report to your direct supervisor and complete the E.U.Z Incident Report Form in the HSE Management System.

## 5. Emergency Response

**Fire:**
1. Raise the alarm immediately.
2. Evacuate to the designated muster point.
3. Do NOT re-enter until the all-clear is given by the Fire Warden.

**Medical Emergency:**
1. Call the site emergency number.
2. Do not move a seriously injured person unless in immediate danger.
3. Trained first-aiders will respond within 4 minutes on all E.U.Z sites.

**Spill / Release:**
1. Isolate the source if safe to do so.
2. Contain using spill kit.
3. Notify HSE team immediately.
4. Do not dispose of contaminated material — await HSE guidance.
